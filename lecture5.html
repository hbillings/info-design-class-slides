Next steps: data wrangling

  • Get very comfortable with statistics.
  • Learn how to handle large datasets (probably with Python, csvkit, other tools).
  • Understand standard practices for data on the web (JSON, APIs, AJAX calls)
  • Practice getting data, cleaning data, and putting it online.

Resources: data wrangling

  • "Statistics Unplugged," Sally Caldwell
  • Listservs: PythonJournos | RJournos | NICAR-L
  • Texas Tribune News Apps blog
  • Small Data Journalism
  • Data Journalism Handbook

Next steps: design & presentation

  • Develop your critical eye. Ask why certain patterns are successful. Save things you like (and don't).
  • When possible, look at the data behind graphics.
  • Delve into UX/UI and interaction design principles
  • Figure out which tools you like for prototyping and production and learn them well (Balsamiq, Illustrator, JavaScript)
  • Look at complex visualizations on various devices to see what decisions the designer made.

Resources: design & presentation

  • Sidebar
  • Apple's human interaction guidelines for mobile
  • Google's Material Design
  • Information is Beautiful
  • BuzzFeed Product blog

Next steps: web development

  • Pick a stack and learn it deeply. Don't technology-hop without a good reason.
  • Consider learning how to build natively.
  • Learn how the front end and the back end communicate with and rely on each other.

Resources: web development

  • Learn Python the Hard Way
  • JavaScript: The Good Parts
